About the Foundation

The Pojulu Heritage Foundation is a community-driven initiative dedicated to preserving, documenting, and celebrating the history, culture, and legacy of the Pojulu people of Lainya County, Central Equatoria State, South Sudan.

"Our elders are living archives. When they pass without being recorded, a library burns. The Pojulu Heritage Foundation exists to ensure no more libraries burn."

— Pojulu Heritage Foundation Mission Statement
Who We Are

Preserving Pojulu for Eternity

The Pojulu people are an indigenous community of Central Equatoria State, South Sudan. Their homeland is Lainya County, with Lainya Centre as the administrative headquarters and main settlements at Kupera, Kenyi, Wuji, and Mukaya. Additional Pojulu communities live in Wonduruba in Juba County and in parts of Yei County. With a population of over 200,000 — including a significant diaspora across Uganda, Kenya, Europe, and beyond — the Pojulu carry one of the richest and most peaceful cultural traditions in South Sudan.

The Pojulu are known throughout South Sudan as a people of peace — a tradition of harmonious coexistence with all 64 ethnic communities in the country that has endured across generations. The annual Pojulu Cultural Festival, whose theme is "Our Culture, Our Strength: Inspiring Unity Across Generations", draws 25,000–30,000 participants and has been recognised by the national Ministry of Culture as a model for cultural preservation.

Yet this heritage is at risk. Elders who carry clan histories, genealogies, traditional ceremonies, songs, proverbs, and oral traditions are passing away without being recorded. The Pojulu Heritage Foundation was created in direct response to this urgency — to ensure that no Pojulu child, wherever they are born, grows up without knowing where they come from.

Our Vision

"No Pojulu child born 100 years from today will have to wonder where they came from, who their ancestors were, or what cultural legacy they inherited."

Three Pillars of Work

📋
I. Historical Documentation
Systematic collection of Pojulu origins, migration routes, clan histories, traditional leadership, cultural practices, ceremonies, songs, proverbs, and oral literature — gathered through elder interviews, church records, and community research.
🌳
II. Genealogy Archive
Family trees, clan records, lineage maps, birth and death records where available, and oral histories linked directly to specific families — so that future generations can trace their ancestry several generations back with confidence.
🏛️
III. Heritage Centre
A physical museum and reading room in Lainya County (Phase III), and a live digital platform accessible worldwide — preserving historical documents, photographs, traditional artefacts, lineage charts, elder recordings, and educational materials for schools.
